Corporate Income Tax Rates

Federal and provincial corporate tax rates for Canadian-controlled private corporations (CCPCs). Includes general and small business rates.

Federal Corporate Rates

Income TypeRateThreshold
General active business income15.00%No limit
Small business deduction (active income)9.00%First $500,000
Investment income (aggregate)38.67%Refundable portion: 30.67%

The small business deduction is available on the first $500,000 of active business income for CCPCPs. The limit is reduced if taxable capital employed in Canada exceeds $10 million and is fully eliminated at $50 million. The business limit must be shared among associated corporations.

Investment Income in a CCPC

Investment income earned inside a corporation is taxed at high rates to discourage tax deferral. The federal rate on aggregate investment income is 38.67%, of which 30.67% is refundable when the corporation pays taxable dividends. Combined federal-provincial rates on investment income typically exceed 50%.

The refundable tax mechanism is designed to achieve integration: when the corporation pays a dividend, the refundable tax is returned to the corporation through the dividend refund, so the total tax approximates what an individual would have paid directly.

SBD Phase-Out for Large CCPCPs

If your corporation and associated corporations have taxable capital employed in Canada between $10 million and $50 million, the small business deduction is gradually phased out. The business limit is reduced by $5 for every $1 of taxable capital above $10 million. Planning is essential for groups approaching these thresholds.
